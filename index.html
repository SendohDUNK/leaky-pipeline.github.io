THINKING

The Leaky Pipeline
Throughtout Life

The term "leaky pipeline" is a concept in the field of scientometrics, which refers to the fact that women are more likely to quit than men at every stage of the education process. This seems to be not only a concept of science of science.

The gender inequality we feel in our daily lives does not exist in isolation, but is the result of institutionalized inequality.

As the schematic diagram on the right illustrates, women are subject to explicit or implicit constraints in a series of areas of their lives, and when each and every element of the pipeline leaks, ultimately what emerges is an immense overall inequality.

Life is out there waiting for you. But can you get past the front door?

ratio: 0%

Let's start simulating a female who is eligible to enter college now. At many stages, women may encounter difficulties that they would not usually pay attention to. At each stage, we ask you to drag the slider on the left to answer our questions, and we will provide the correct answer to verify your impression. First, can we move freely? This data comes from the World Bank, which measures a country's restrictions on women's movements through four questions. Question 1: Can a woman choose where to live in the same way as a man? Question 2: Can a woman travel outside her home in the same way as a man? Question 3: Can a woman apply for a passport in the same way as a man? Question 4: Can a woman travel outside the country in the same way as a man?
Now, how many countries do you think have more restrictive limits on women's freedom of movement?
